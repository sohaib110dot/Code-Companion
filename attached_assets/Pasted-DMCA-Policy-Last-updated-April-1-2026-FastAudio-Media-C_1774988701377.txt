Required Information in Your Notice
Your DMCA notice must contain all of the following elements to be valid under 17 U.S.C. § 512(c)(3):

Physical or electronic signature of the copyright owner or authorized person
Identification of the copyrighted work(s) you claim has been infringed
Identification of the infringing material — the URL(s) submitted through our Service
Your contact information: full name, mailing address, telephone number, and email address
Good-faith belief statement: "I have a good faith belief that the use of the material is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law."
Accuracy statement under penalty of perjury that you are the copyright owner or authorized to act on their behalf
Warning: Under Section 512(f) of the DMCA, any person who knowingly misrepresents that material is infringing may be subject to liability for damages. Do not submit false DMCA notices.

Our Response Process
Upon receipt of a complete and valid DMCA notice, we will:

Acknowledge receipt of your complaint within 3 business days
Review the notice for completeness and validity
Block the reported URL(s) from being processed through our Service
Notify the user who submitted the URL of the blocking action (where possible)
Counter-Notice Procedure
If you believe that content was incorrectly blocked as a result of a DMCA notice, you may send a counter-notice. A valid counter-notice must contain:

Your physical or electronic signature
Identification of the material that was removed
A statement under penalty of perjury that you have a good faith belief the material was removed by mistake
Your name, address, telephone number, and a statement consenting to jurisdiction
